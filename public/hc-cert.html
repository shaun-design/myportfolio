Phase 1 • months 0 to 6
CPB + CPC
Fastest practical entry path with no formal degree required. CPC often takes 4 to 6 months. CPB can be added in roughly 1 to 2 more months. A more realistic first year target is around the low to mid 50s, with stronger upside after experience.
Phase 2 • months 6 to 18
CRC
Strong follow on specialization once you have coding fundamentals. Risk adjustment work rewards pattern recognition, hierarchy logic, and comfort with structured data.
Phase 3 • year 2 to 3
RCM analyst or specialist track
This is where process improvement, dashboards, denial trends, and systems thinking become more valuable. It is a better place to aim for higher compensation than trying to force a first year salary estimate too high.
Important nuance: CPB and CPC are real entry options. CRC is a stronger second move. CHDA, CHPS, CCS, and especially CCDS are better understood as later stage options because of experience or clinical barriers.
Note: Salary, remote percentages, and AI risk labels are best treated as directional estimates. Hiring market, geography, apprenticeship status, and employer mix can move these up or down materially.
