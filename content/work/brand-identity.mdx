## Overview

A B2B SaaS company approached me to build their brand from scratch. They had a strong engineering team, a working product, and zero design foundation. My job was to fix that — and to deliver something the team could actually ship from.

## The Challenge

The company needed:

- A brand identity that felt credible and modern without being generic
- A design system their frontend team could implement directly in code
- Documentation thorough enough to onboard future designers and engineers

## Process

### Discovery

I started with a three-week discovery phase: stakeholder interviews, a competitive audit, and sessions with a handful of their customers. The key insight was that their buyers were operations teams — people who care about trust, efficiency, and not having to think too hard.

That shaped everything that came after.

### Brand Identity

Drawing on that research, I developed an identity around three principles:

1. **Clarity** — clean typography, high contrast, minimal decoration
2. **Structure** — consistent spacing and grid-based layouts that signal reliability
3. **Confidence** — a strong color palette anchored by a deep indigo primary

After two rounds of review, the direction was locked.

### Design System

Once the brand was approved, I translated it into a Figma component library with ~80 components across 12 categories. I worked alongside the engineering team to implement it in React with Tailwind CSS, writing component documentation in parallel.

## Results

- Launched on schedule, three months after kickoff
- Design system adopted by a team of 8 engineers across two product squads
- Reduced design-to-dev handoff time by approximately 60% by end of Q2

## What I Learned

Building a design system from scratch is as much organizational work as it is design work. The tokens matter. The naming conventions matter. The documentation — especially the "why we made this decision" parts — matters more than people expect.
